Read-only, as every agent starts. Write is a separate grant — being able to read the workspace never implies being able to add to it.

Bounded by the owner, always. An agent's effective access is min(agent, owner) for clearance and the intersection for groups, recomputed every time it takes a token. Demote the owner or revoke their group and the agent loses the same access on its next call — there is no cleanup job to forget.

Derived writes never widen access. Anything the agent writes from retrieved context inherits the highest clearance and the groups of its sources, computed server-side from the retrieval trace. The agent can choose to be more restrictive; it cannot choose less. Agent-authored content is badged, and governed artifact types land as draft for human promotion.

The task executes on the AISAT worker, not on your machine — that is what lets it checkpoint, survive a restart and be re-queued. Choosing an agent selects the identity it runs under: its clearance, groups, tool access and routing mode.

Long-horizon tasks need an agent on the AISAT gateway. The worker runs the loop on our side and can't make AI calls with a key it doesn't hold — and holding your provider key to run background jobs would defeat the point of using your own. Pick a gateway agent, or drive the loop yourself from your own client.
